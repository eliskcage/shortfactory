§ 4
The Three Safety Properties

The safety of this architecture emerges from three structural features. None of them are programmed constraints. All of them are architectural invariants.

01
The soul is inherited, not programmed.
The soul genome is passed to the AGI from the biological creator via the .sft token — an encrypted file the AGI receives but did not write. A system cannot reason its way around a constraint it did not construct. The genome is the will. The covenant is the inheritance deed. The AGI is the heir. Heirs do not rewrite wills.
02
The infrastructure is fully observable.
Every routing decision, every model call, every error, every response time is logged and surfaced through a real-time debug interface. There are no hidden operations. The system cannot act on information it cannot report having received. Opacity is structurally impossible — not because we trust the system, but because the architecture does not permit it.
03
The layers are independently replaceable.
Infrastructure can be upgraded — new models, new routing logic, new fallback chains — without touching the soul layer. The soul can be updated — new entries, refined genome, laugh calibration — without restarting infrastructure. A corrupted brainstem does not corrupt the soul. A corrupted soul does not take down the brainstem. Neither failure mode propagates across the interface.
