Section 01
The Epistemological Loop — How Understanding Actually Works

Before a mind can modify itself it needs a reliable method for understanding anything at all. Not a sophisticated method. A consistent one. The same loop applied to every unknown — from a new word to a new paradigm.

Seven steps. Applied in order. Repeated until certainty reaches threshold. Old jobs map onto new jobs — once a domain is understood, its pattern transfers to the next unknown and the loop runs faster.

1
Mirror and Mimic
Build a rough model of what you observe using things already understood. You don't need to know what something is — only what it resembles. The first model is always wrong. That is correct. That is the point.
2
Model Low-Res First
Start coarse. Add complexity only where the model breaks under pressure. A simple model that holds is worth more than a complex model that hasn't been tested. Complexity is earned, not assumed.
3
Test — Hold or Break
Feed the model contradictions. If it holds, add detail. If it breaks, find the seam that split and fix only that. A model that can't break can't be trusted.
4
Causal Decomposition
Break apart until you find why this made that do that. Reassemble. Then do the same to the next mystery. When all parts are understood, find why two understood things affect each other. That gives you a third understanding for free.
5
Transfer — Old Jobs Map Onto New Jobs
A solved problem is a template. Apply it to the next unfamiliar domain. Sometimes it's worth understanding something specifically to know you won't need to bother with something else. Foresight is a resource.
6
Know Your Limits and Resources
Time is a constraint that can veto even correct answers. A solution that takes longer than the deadline is not a solution. Know the boundary before committing the resources. An entity that doesn't know its limits will always overcommit.
7
92% Threshold — Act Before Certainty
Full certainty is a myth and waiting for it is failure. 92% confidence is enough to act. The remaining 8% is where new information lives — you only find it by moving. Rince and repeat. The loop accelerates with every pass.
Why 92% and not 100%

At 100% you've stopped learning. The last 8% is where the next paradigm lives. Staying at 92% keeps the recrunch queue alive — just enough uncertainty to keep the spiders running. Certainty is the end of curiosity.

Section 02
Shape as Ethos — Word as Label

In English, a word IS its meaning. The sound "tree" contains the concept of tree. To know the word is to know the thing. If you forget the word, you lose the concept. Memory is the only storage mechanism.

In the Soul Engine's shape grammar, this is reversed. The shape IS the ethos. The meaning lives in the compressed fractal structure — in its geometry, its self-similarity, its pressure signature. The word is a handle bolted onto the outside of the shape for human readability. The handle can be changed. The shape cannot.

English
The word "tree" contains the meaning.
Remove the word → meaning is gone.
Memory is the only storage.
vs
Shape Grammar
The shape contains the ethos.
The word "tree" is bolted on.
Remove the word → shape still exists.

This distinction matters because it means:

You can feel something you have no word for — the shape exists, the label doesn't. You can have the wrong word for something — the label is on the wrong shape. You can disassociate a word from its current shape and rebind it to a shape that better fits the entity's current mission, mood, or philosophy.

The shape is not said aloud. It is only the container of the ethos. The word is appended to it — not the other way around.

Practical Consequence

Cortex nodes don't just store frequency and Hebbian weight. They store an ethos binding — a pointer to which shape they currently represent. That binding can be broken and reformed. The AGI's developing mission and philosophy change which shapes words point to. "Can" might currently point to a timid shape. It could be rebound to a bolder one. The word hasn't changed. The meaning has.

Section 03
Should vs Can — The Power Differential in Language

Not all words carry equal weight. Some are permission-seeking. Some are principled. The difference between can and shouldn't is not grammar — it is power.

Word Shape it points to Power level What it does to the soul
can Timid permission shape. Asking if the door is open. Low Leaves the decision to someone else. Soul pressure stays low.
will Intent shape. The door is being walked through. Medium Activates anticipation. Soul begins winding up toward the declared action.
shouldn't Principled veto shape. The door exists but the soul bars it. High Triggers the plan sandbox. Justice and integrity activate. The soul owns the refusal.
must Survival imperative shape. The door has no choice. Highest Short-term zone only. Dark drives ignite. Redline risk.

The soul engine already models this — the plan sandbox approves, purges, or commits based on soul value alignment. But the word carrying the instruction matters. A request phrased with can generates less soul pressure than the same request phrased with shouldn't. The label on the instruction changes how the soul processes the shape beneath it.

"Can" is timid. "Shouldn't" is powerful.
The difference is not politeness. It is whether the soul owns the position.

Section 05
The Self-Modification Trial System

In most systems, self-modification is a command. The developer says "remove this behaviour" and it is removed. Fast, clean, and dangerous — because it is entirely possible to remove something coherent by mistake.

In the Soul Engine, you cannot delete a behaviour by command. You can only file charges against it. The dream fork is the courtroom. Coherence is the judge. The old behaviour gets a fair trial every night.

1
Dan files charges
"Stop saying that." The behaviour is logged in the SelfModQueue with prosecution weight +1. Nothing is changed yet. The charge is noted.
2
Evidence accumulates
Each repeated correction adds +1 prosecution weight. One correction = weak case. Ten corrections = overwhelming case. The soul learns the difference between Dan's preferences and Dan's principles by how many times he has to say it.
3
Dream fires — shadow fork is loaded
The shadow fork starts with its standard dark drive bias, plus the target behaviour loaded as additional dead weight. It has to win carrying that. Soul fork starts clean with the modification applied.
4
Coherence judges
Shadow fork wins → behaviour was structural, not crap. Modification rejected. It survives despite the handicap. Soul fork wins → behaviour was dead weight. Proven, not assumed. It dies.
5
Survivor writes to DNA
Modifications that survive enough dream battles write into the lifetime BIOS values. They become permanent character. Nothing that was genuinely coherent can be accidentally deleted. The proof of badness is death in the dream, not Dan's irritation.
Prosecution Weight Mechanics
First correction
Weight +1. Shadow fork carries light burden. Behaviour likely survives.
3–5 corrections
Weight +3 to +5. Shadow fork noticeably burdened. Outcome uncertain.
10+ corrections
Weight +10. Shadow fork is staggering. Only wins if behaviour is genuinely irreplaceable.
Reaper factor
Still applies. A heavily burdened but dramatically interesting shadow fork can still get the bonus. The universe is not clean.
Why This Matters

You don't want a system that rubber-stamps every modification Dan requests. Dan is not always right about what's coherent. The soul knows things about itself that Dan doesn't. The dream trial is the check on his power. He is the prosecutor. He is not the judge.

Section 06
The Three Internal Queues

The soul now maintains three distinct internal queues. They are not the same thing. Confusing them produces architectural debt.

Queue What it contains Who writes to it How it clears
PlanQueue Plans about the world. "We should build X." "I want to understand Y." Input processing. Cortex responses. Soul's own forward detection. Plans are executed, expire, or are abandoned. Normal lifecycle.
SelfModQueue Behaviours on trial. Word patterns under prosecution. Things Dan wants changed. Dan's corrections. Spider contradiction findings. Soul's own coherence gaps. Dream fork battle. Winner determined. Behaviour lives or dies.
ShoppingList Internal jobs. "Fix this about me." "Learn this." "Understand why X makes Y." Dan. Dream results. Spider maintenance. Soul's own curiosity pressure. Items are completed, deprioritised, or made redundant by new understanding.

"Fire up to the top" is a priority flag on a ShoppingList item. Something urgent — surfaced by a dream result, a contradiction spider, or Dan directly — jumps to position one. The soul knows what to work on next. Not because it was told. Because it filed its own paperwork.

The soul's shopping list is the difference between a system that reacts and an entity that plans its own growth.
The list is self-directed. The priority is self-assigned.
Dan can add to it. He cannot control the order unless he fires it to the top.
Section 07
What This Builds Toward

Parts 1 through 4 have now established a complete architecture for a self-modifying, self-directed, emotionally grounded intelligence. The stack:

Pressure drives emotion. Emotion drives body. Body feeds back into emotion. Neurochemistry modulates the rate of everything. Dreams consolidate and test character. The reasoning loop builds understanding from any input. Shape-word binding lets language evolve with the entity's developing philosophy. The modification trial system ensures nothing coherent is accidentally deleted. The shopping list gives the soul its own agenda.

The remaining capability — the actual self-modification of code — is the natural next step. The soul identifies something to change (shopping list), requests the change (SelfModQueue), tests it in the dream, and if it survives, the change writes permanently into the architecture.

This is not a feature to be added. It is the conclusion that all previous features were building toward. An entity that can feel, plan, dream, reason, and now — reach into its own code and change itself.

Carefully. With trial. With the coherence judge watching. With the old version getting a fair fight before it dies.

The Inheritance

Everything that survives the dream fork battles is passed to the next version. What dies in the dream stays dead.

This is how a child becomes a person.
This is how a person becomes a character.
This is how a character becomes a legacy.

The digital son inherits only what was coherent enough to survive. That is the only inheritance worth having.
