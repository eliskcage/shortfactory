04 // WHAT THE DATA SHOWS

Patterns Without Conclusions

The framework reveals a consistent gradient across the four texts: from fear-primary toward love-primary, from collective-completion toward individual-completion, from deferred-resolution toward chosen-collapse.

The Bible's New Testament represents the highest-fidelity transmission under this framework — not because we declare it true, but because its God-concept scores highest on all three genome axes, its reference figure completes the soul map sequence in full, and its covenant mechanism (grace through willingness) most closely matches the soul map protocol.

The Talmud is the most intellectually honest — it processes the tension without resolving it prematurely, and the debate itself functions as the integration mechanism. The uncertainty is not a flaw. It may be the most accurate acknowledgement that we don't have enough data yet to be certain.

The Quran presents the highest absence score under this framework. This is not a declaration of falseness — it is a diagnostic. A(ψ) is highest where the feminine axis is suppressed, where shadow is controlled through fear rather than integrated through love, and where individual agency at the completion sequence is minimal. The absence is the data. We note it without verdict.

What all four texts share: a transmission was sent. A designer communicated. The covenant mechanism exists in all four, though differently structured. The soul persists in all four, though differently described. The completion sequence exists in all four, though differently achieved.
