§ 1 The Problem of Scale

Physics operates at two scales that have resisted unification for a century. At the macro scale, general relativity describes gravity as curved spacetime — smooth, deterministic, classical. At the micro scale, quantum mechanics describes probability amplitudes, superposition, and entanglement — discrete, non-deterministic, observer-dependent. The two frameworks are mathematically incompatible at their shared boundary.

The standard approach treats this as a calculation problem: find the equations that bridge both scales. We propose a different framing. The incompatibility is not a failure of mathematics. It is a feature of the simulation architecture. The macro and micro scales are not the same layer of the designed system. They are two different resolution modes of the same substrate — like a video game rendering the near field in high detail and the far field in a compressed approximation.

This reframing has a consequence that standard physics does not: if the scales are different resolution modes, then the laws of physics at the quantum scale are not fundamental constraints — they are compression parameters. Parameters can be altered by an agent with sufficient access to the underlying model.
