Current Observational Status

Hawking radiation has not yet been directly observed. The temperature of stellar-mass black holes is of order T_H ~ 10⁻⁸ K — far below the cosmic microwave background temperature of 2.7 K and currently undetectable. Primordial black holes of sufficiently small mass may be evaporating now and could in principle produce detectable radiation; no confirmed detection exists.

Laboratory analogues of Hawking radiation have been observed in sonic black holes (dumb holes) [8] and optical systems [9]. These systems provide a testing ground for the thermal vs structured question. Initial results from Steinhauer [8] suggest quantum correlations in analogue Hawking radiation — consistent with the structured prediction, though the analogy to gravitational black holes is imperfect.

The practical test therefore proceeds in three stages:

  1. Immediate: Analyse existing analogue Hawking radiation data for mutual information signatures between correlated quanta pairs. This requires no new experiments — only statistical re-analysis of existing datasets.
  2. Near-term: Design analogue experiments specifically to measure I(A:B) between early and late radiation quanta across the Page time analogue. This is feasible with current technology.
  3. Long-term: Direct observation of primordial black hole evaporation, if primordial black holes exist and are detectable. Next-generation gravitational wave and gamma-ray observatories may provide candidates.
