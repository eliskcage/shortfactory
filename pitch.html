The Problem With Every Neural Network

Every neural network on the planet learns the same way: make a guess, measure the error, push the correction backwards through every layer. Repeat billions of times. The result works — but it produces a system that is opaque, fragile, unable to explain itself, and physically capable of holding false beliefs indefinitely.

These are not engineering problems. They are architectural ones.

Left: every major AI system. Right: SphereNet. The difference is the floor, not the furniture.

A Different Architecture

SphereNet replaces error correction with physics. Every concept is a sphere in embedding space — a point with a radius, a groove counter, and a decay timer. Learning is not adjustment. It is gravity.

A signal arrives. Nearby spheres resonate. Each resonating sphere drifts toward the signal by 8% — not snapping to it, leaning. Over thousands of signals, the lean becomes the concept's position. No error. No gradient. No loss function. Just physics.

Five mechanical laws govern everything: Resonance, Drift, Groove, Smart Timer, Merge. That is the entire architecture.
