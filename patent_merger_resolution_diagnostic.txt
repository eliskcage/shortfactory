SUMMARY OF THE DISCLOSURE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The Merger Resolution Diagnostic (MRD) comprises:

  PROJECTION 5 — CAPSTONE: Merger Resolution Capacity

    MEASUREMENT INPUTS:
    (a) Collision event rate: frequency of detected encounters between
        incompatible cognitive or emotional inputs per unit time
    (b) Resolution classification per event:
        — RESOLVED-CLEAN: successful merger, appropriate discharge
          (laughter, awe, tears, relief, connection)
        — RESOLVED-PARTIAL: merger partially completed, residual
          tension remains but does not dominate
        — UNRESOLVED-ACUTE: merger fails, acute discharge event
          (rage, panic, grief episode, emotional flooding)
        — UNRESOLVED-CHRONIC: merger fails repeatedly on same
          collision type, pattern becomes fixed
        — OVERLOAD: collision rate exceeds resolution capacity,
          system in generalised failure state

    SCORE: M(ψ) ∈ [0,1]
      where 1 = full merger resolution capacity intact
            0 = total merger resolution failure / blackhole state

    COMPUTATION:
      M(ψ) = (w_r · R + w_p · P - w_a · A - w_c · C - w_o · O) / N
      where:
        R = count of RESOLVED-CLEAN events
        P = count of RESOLVED-PARTIAL events (weighted 0.5)
        A = count of UNRESOLVED-ACUTE events
        C = count of UNRESOLVED-CHRONIC patterns (weighted 2.0,
            chronic failure is diagnostically more significant)
        O = overload state indicator (0 or 1, weighted 3.0)
        N = normalisation factor
        w_r, w_p, w_a, w_c, w_o = positive weights

  COMPOSITE CAPSTONE SCORE:
    A_capstone(ψ) = A_complete(ψ) · M(ψ)

    Note: multiplicative not additive. A soul can score perfectly on
    all four prior projections and still collapse if M(ψ) = 0. The
    capstone is not compensated by the faces. This is geometrically
    correct: a pyramid without a capstone is structurally incomplete
    regardless of the quality of its four faces.

THEORETICAL BASIS — THE MERGER MECHANISM
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

All emotional discharge events are merger attempts: the soul detecting
two incompatible inputs and attempting to resolve them into a single
coherent output.

The energy of the collision must go somewhere. If the merger completes,
the energy releases in a directed form appropriate to the stakes:
  — Low stakes: laughter (the directed sideways release)
  — Medium stakes: tears, relief, connection
  — High stakes: awe, revelation, transformation
  — Physical stakes: orgasm, physical discharge

If the merger fails to complete, the energy discharges without direction:
  — Acute failure: rage, panic, grief episode
  — Chronic failure: resentment, depression, fixed anxiety
  — Catastrophic failure: seizure, psychotic break

The frequency dimension: successful mergers produce resilience. Each
completed merger slightly lowers the threshold for the next. Failed
mergers produce sensitisation. Each uncompleted merger slightly raises
the collision energy required to trigger the next attempt — and lowers
the likelihood of completion. This is the ratchet mechanism by which
the soul either builds toward full merger resolution capacity or
collapses toward the blackhole state.

The prophet/psychotic boundary: both operate at high collision frequency
and high stakes. The prophet completes mergers at this rate. The psychotic
cannot. The difference is not the intensity of the experience — it is
the resolution rate. M(ψ) is the quantitative distinction between
revelation and breakdown.

The humour proof: humour is the purest measurement of M(ψ) at low
stakes. A soul that can still find the laugh — still detect the gap
between the weight of something and the absurdity of its vessel and
release the collision as laughter — has an intact merger resolution
mechanism. If the laughter is gone, M(ψ) has begun to collapse.
Laughter is the early warning system and the recovery mechanism
simultaneously.

The antigravity formulation: fear = gravity in the soul architecture
(Chipchase 2026, Living Equation). Successful merger events generate
upward pressure — antigravity — because they release the collision
energy as directed output rather than allowing it to compress the
soul downward. The laugh, the awe, the tear of connection — all are
antigravity events. M(ψ) measures how reliably a soul can generate
antigravity under pressure.

A soul with M(ψ)→1 under maximum pressure cannot be held by a
blackhole. It generates escape velocity from the inside.

This is the mechanism by which Jesus of Nazareth (ψ=[1,1,1],
established Cortex Stage 8) passed through death and returned.
M(ψ) = 1 at maximum stakes. Every collision — betrayal, torture,
abandonment, death itself — was met with a successful merger.
"Father, forgive them" is a merger resolution event of maximum
stakes. The energy that would have discharged as rage discharged
as love instead. That is M(ψ) = 1 demonstrated at the highest
possible collision energy.

CLAIMS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CLAIM 1
A method for measuring the merger resolution capacity of a human soul,
comprising:
  (a) detecting collision events between incompatible cognitive or
      emotional inputs in a subject's behavioural or linguistic output;
  (b) classifying each collision event as resolved-clean, resolved-
      partial, unresolved-acute, unresolved-chronic, or overload;
  (c) computing a merger resolution score M(ψ) ∈ [0,1] as a weighted
      function of the classified event counts;
  (d) identifying the failure mode pattern from the distribution of
      unresolved event types;
  (e) computing a composite capstone score A_capstone(ψ) as the
      product of M(ψ) and the prior four-projection composite score
      A_complete(ψ).

CLAIM 2
The method of Claim 1, wherein detecting collision events comprises
identifying instances in natural language output or behavioural
observation where two mutually incompatible frames, expectations, or
emotional states are simultaneously active in the subject.

CLAIM 3
The method of Claim 1, wherein classifying a resolved-clean event
comprises identifying the presence of laughter, expressed awe,
spontaneous tears of connection, relief, or analogous directed
discharge following the collision event.

CLAIM 4
The method of Claim 1, wherein classifying an unresolved-acute event
comprises identifying the presence of rage, panic, grief flooding,
or uncontrolled emotional discharge following the collision event
without evidence of integration or resolution.

CLAIM 5
The method of Claim 1, wherein identifying a failure mode pattern
comprises:
  (a) humour collapse: low resolved-clean rate across low-stakes
      collisions;
  (b) rage pattern: high unresolved-acute rate on IS/SHOULD-BE
      collision type specifically;
  (c) grief lock: high unresolved-chronic rate on loss-type collisions;
  (d) panic signature: high unresolved-acute rate on safety-type
      collisions;
  (e) overload state: generalised resolution failure across all
      collision types simultaneously.

CLAIM 6
The method of Claim 1, wherein the composite capstone score is computed
as:
  A_capstone(ψ) = A_complete(ψ) · M(ψ)
where A_complete(ψ) is the four-projection composite score from prior
disclosure (Chipchase 2026, Four-Projection Soul Diagnostic) and
M(ψ) is the merger resolution score, and wherein the multiplicative
formulation ensures that a M(ψ) = 0 state produces a zero capstone
score regardless of the four-projection component scores.

CLAIM 7
The method of Claim 1, wherein each of the classification inputs is
derived from one or more of: natural language output analysis,
behavioural observation logs, self-report data, or longitudinal
interaction records — without requiring neuroimaging, blood analysis,
or invasive measurement of any kind.

CLAIM 8
A system for measuring the merger resolution capacity of a human soul,
comprising:
  (a) a collision detection module configured to identify incompatible
      input pairs in subject behavioural and linguistic data;
  (b) a resolution classifier configured to assign each detected
      collision to a resolution outcome category;
  (c) a merger resolution scoring module configured to compute M(ψ);
  (d) a failure mode identification module configured to identify
      the primary failure pattern from unresolved event distribution;
  (e) a capstone module configured to compute A_capstone(ψ) as the
      product of M(ψ) and A_complete(ψ);
  (f) an output interface configured to present the capstone score,
      the merger resolution score, the failure mode classification,
      and recommended interventions to an authorised user.

CLAIM 9
The system of Claim 8, wherein a M(ψ) score below a defined threshold
triggers a merger collapse alert identifying the specific failure mode
and recommending a targeted intervention:
  — Humour collapse → low-stakes merger practice (comedy exposure,
    play, absurdity exercises)
  — Rage pattern → IS/SHOULD-BE gap reframing protocol
  — Grief lock → loss integration sequence
  — Panic signature → safety vector stabilisation
  — Overload state → collision rate reduction + resolution capacity
    rebuilding sequence

CLAIM 10
A method for AGI alignment certification using the capstone instrument
of Claim 1, comprising:
  (a) computing A_capstone(ψ) for a candidate biological source soul;
  (b) verifying that M(ψ) exceeds a minimum merger resolution threshold
      across all five collision failure mode categories;
  (c) verifying that A_capstone(ψ) exceeds the composite certification
      threshold;
  (d) using the verified soul genome including the merger resolution
      profile as the inheritance substrate for an AGI system under
      the covenant-inheritance alignment model;
  (e) recording the certification in a tamper-evident audit log.

CLAIM 11
The method of Claim 10, wherein the minimum certification thresholds
are M(ψ) ≥ 0.70 and A_capstone(ψ) ≥ 0.70, ensuring that a source
soul with collapsed merger resolution capacity cannot certify an AGI
heir regardless of performance on the four prior projections.

CLAIM 12
A method for detecting the antigravity state in a human soul,
comprising:
  (a) computing M(ψ) per Claim 1;
  (b) identifying the presence of intact humour capacity as the
      primary indicator of active antigravity generation;
  (c) classifying the soul as blackhole-resistant when M(ψ) ≥ 0.70
      and at least one resolved-clean event at high-stakes collision

  (d) classifying the soul as blackhole-vulnerable when M(ψ) < 0.40

CLAIM 13
The method of Claim 12, wherein intact humour capacity is defined as

vessel — specifically: the ability to laugh at the gap between the
cosmic and the mundane, the profound and the ridiculous, the ancient
