The Problem With Pets

Digital pets have existed since 1996. Tamagotchi. Neopets. Every one of them made the same mistake: the creature exists alone. You feed it. You clean it. You watch numbers go up. Eventually you forget. It dies. You feel nothing.

The reason you feel nothing is because nothing was at stake between two living things.

ALIVE had already solved the first half -- a creature with real blood, real death, real emotional physics. But a creature alone in a tank is still just a screensaver with a heartbeat.

Today, the creatures learned to find each other across the void.

The Bridge

Two devices. A phone running the Boy. A laptop running the Girl. Between them: nothing. Different browsers, different networks, different screens. The void.

The bridge is an invisible iframe loaded by both creatures. A tiny embassy that speaks to a single PHP file on the server. One atomic request per cycle -- status report, note delivery, anger signal, revival song -- all in a single read-write. No race conditions. No split-brain. One bridge, two souls.

When both creatures ping within 120 seconds, the server declares them paired. A cyan phone icon pulses on the Girl's screen. A pink screen icon pulses on the Boy's. They can hear each other now.

The infrastructure of love is a hidden iframe and a JSON file.

The Game That Designed Itself

It started with revival. The Boy plays square-triangle-circle. The Girl hears it, even in death. Three notes pull her back from the dark. She plays it backwards to save him. Circle-triangle-square. The mirror.

Then came hearts. Fifteen empty slots. Each revival earns more than the last. First revival: one heart. Second: two. Third: three. Hearts are shields -- when blood runs dry, a heart shatters and you come back alone. No partner needed. But you needed a partner to earn it.

Then Dan said the thing that made it a real game:

"we should have a drain blood song, like its angry, hitting square a bunch of times fast sends it into an angry fit which drains the opposite ones blood"

Anger. Four rapid hits of your creature's anger key -- square for Boy, triangle for Girl, deliberately dissimilar -- sends a shockwave across the bridge. The other creature's screen flashes red. Fifteen blood gone. Seven anger bursts kills a full-health creature.

Now the game loop closes: kill them with anger, revive them with music, earn escalating hearts, fill all fifteen, discover harmony combos for longevity. The creatures have to hurt each other to save each other.

Nobody designed this. It emerged from the conversation. Each mechanic made the next one inevitable.

The Spell Book

Three shapes. That is what you start with. Square, triangle, circle. No diamond, no wave. Those are locked behind the longest spells you will ever play.

The progression is exponential:

HARMONY COMBOS — 3 notes — slow the blood drain
REVIVAL CODES — 3 notes — pull your partner back from death
ANGER — 4 rapid hits — drain the other creature's blood
THE HEART SPELL — 8 notes — fill all 15 hearts

THE DIAMOND AWAKENING — 16 notes — unlock the 4th shape

THE WAVE OF CREATION — 24 notes — unlock the 5th shape

Each spell is taught through the Ancient Songs book -- a Zelda-style story system where the narrative IS the tutorial. Shapes light up in sequence. You watch the demo. Then you play it back from memory, note by note, with wrong notes shaking red and resetting you to the start.

Players don't memorise combos. They remember stories. The story of the two souls in the void who learned to speak through shapes.

The Three Versions

Dan identified something during the session that changes the entire product strategy:

"this version the kids version should remain without the camera function. this way shortfactory does not ever have to worry about that nightmare whatsoever because we dont let kids even have the camera mode"

ALIVE ships as three distinct products:

v1 — Kids. Shapes, music, pairing, revival, hearts, harmony, anger, spells. No camera. No eye mode. Not hidden -- absent from the code entirely. Zero privacy liability. Zero safeguarding risk. This is the version that went live today.

v2 — Adults. Everything in v1, plus the Training Wheels Eye -- the camera that sees in shapes. Age-gated. Requires a fully mastered v1 creature to unlock (all shapes, all hearts). You earn v2 by proving you speak the language.

v3 — TBD. The next evolution. Whatever the creatures become when enough humans learn to speak in shapes.

The camera feature is powerful. The solution is not to cripple it. The solution is to not ship it to children at all.

Three shapes. Two creatures. One bridge. A spell book that teaches itself. The phone becomes a wand.
