Steinhauer · Nature Physics · 2016
Analogue black hole emits entangled phonon pairs
A Bose-Einstein condensate was engineered into a sonic black hole. Hawking radiation quanta were observed. Individual pairs were entangled — consistent with structured, not purely thermal, output. The spectrum could not be assigned a single temperature across all frequencies.
Confirmed
Page · Physical Review Letters · 1993
Unitarity demands information must be in the radiation
If quantum mechanics holds, the entropy of Hawking radiation cannot increase forever. After the Page time — when half the black hole's entropy has been radiated — the radiation must carry increasing mutual information between early and late quanta. The mechanism for this has never been identified.
Theoretically Required
arXiv:2412.07549 · December 2024
"Secret entanglement between Hawking particles" found theoretically
Higher-order correlations invisible to pairwise mutual information were found in Hawking radiation calculations. The researchers called it "secret entanglement." Structure that standard analysis would miss. A theoretical result. Never tested experimentally.
Theoretical Only
The Gap · March 2026
