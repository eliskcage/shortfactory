ABSTRACT

A unified physical and computational model is disclosed in which gravitational attraction and antigravitational repulsion are described as emergent properties of information compression dynamics. The invention defines gravity as the attractive force arising from information density differentials between regions of space — higher-density regions drawing lower-density regions inward through a compression operation. When a region of space reaches maximum information compression — termed the Singularity Threshold or the Dot State — an inversion event occurs, producing a repulsive burst equivalent to antigravitational force. The oscillation between compression and inversion produces cosmological-scale pulsing behaviour analogous to the observed expansion and contraction cycles of the universe. The model further proposes that the event horizon of a black hole constitutes a perspective boundary in information geometry rather than a physical barrier, and that Hawking radiation represents structured informational output from inversion events within the singularity. A working computational simulation demonstrating spontaneous gravitational clustering, inversion events, and antigravitational repulsion is provided as Exhibit A. The invention has applications in theoretical physics, antigravity propulsion research, cosmological modelling, and artificial general intelligence architecture.

FIELD OF THE INVENTION

This invention relates to a theoretical and computational model unifying gravitational mechanics, antigravitational force generation, and cosmological dynamics under a single information-theoretic principle. The invention further relates to a computational implementation of said model demonstrating emergent gravitational and antigravitational behaviour from two base rules.

BACKGROUND

1. The Unresolved Problem of Gravity

General relativity describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass-energy. While predictively accurate at large scales, this description provides no mechanistic account of why mass curves spacetime. The force carrier of gravity — the graviton — has not been experimentally detected. Quantum gravity remains an unsolved problem in theoretical physics. No unified field theory successfully reconciles gravitational mechanics with quantum mechanics.

2. Antigravity — Theoretically Undefined

No mainstream physical theory provides a coherent mechanism for antigravitational repulsion beyond the cosmological constant (dark energy) and certain speculative extensions of general relativity. Empirical anomalies including the Podkletnov effect and Heim theory calculations remain unexplained and largely uninvestigated due to lack of theoretical framework.

3. The Holographic Principle — Mechanism Unknown

The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy formula establishes that the information content of a black hole scales with its surface area rather than its volume — implying that three-dimensional information is encoded on a two-dimensional surface (the event horizon). This holographic principle lacks a mechanistic explanation: no prior art describes the engine that performs the encoding and projects the output.

4. Prior Art — Computanium (GB2605683.8)

The applicant's prior filing GB2605683.8 ("Computanium: a physical medium constituting a sixth state of matter defined by the merge-with-verifiable-truth-score property") establishes the Computanium framework: a medium in which merge(A, B) produces a child C with a truth score ∈ [0,1]. The present invention extends this framework to provide a physical interpretation of gravity and antigravity as emergent properties of the Computanium merge operation at cosmological scale. GB2605683.8 is cited as prior art; claims in the present application are distinct and non-overlapping.
